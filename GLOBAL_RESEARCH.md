## Applicability model

Every downstream outline must identify which layer it discusses:

1. **Base material:** chemistry, mechanical properties, grade, supply condition, and test sample.
2. **Processed product or fabrication:** plate, section, bar, tube, coating, cut edge, hole, bend, weld, bolt, assembly, and its manufacturing records.
3. **Installed system or asset:** load path, connections, supports, exposure, fire protection, erection state, operation, inspection, maintenance, and local legal context.

A material certificate does not prove an installed structure is safe. A dimensional standard does not prove a pipe is fit for pressure service. A test-method standard does not prove that a real product passed. A coating name does not establish substrate preparation, total system, durability, or field repair. Foreign standards and guidance are reference frameworks only until adopted by the Indonesian regulation, contract, drawing, specification, or responsible professional for the project.

## Evidence grades

| Grade | Meaning | Safe use |
|---|---|---|
| A-ID | Current Indonesian law/regulation or official Indonesian standards record | State public status, title, jurisdiction, visible scope, and regulatory boundary |
| B-STD | Official standards-body catalog, abstract, or standards-developer page | Establish document identity, edition/status, and public scope only |
| C-GOV | Government research, regulator guidance, or recognized technical/industry body | Explain concepts and evidence questions within the stated jurisdiction |
| D-MFR | Manufacturer or product-association guidance | Product/process-specific practice only; disclose author, market, product, and specification limits |
| GATE | Full document, project fact, test, survey, calculation, approval, or professional review still required | Preserve as an explicit unresolved gate; never fill with an assumption |

## Standards-status discipline

- `Berlaku` on a BSN catalog page establishes the catalog status visible on the verification date; it does **not** by itself establish mandatory application to every product, contract, structure, or transaction.
- The BSN catalog can display older and newer editions at the same time. For example, public results show both SNI 2052:2017 and a related SNI 2052:2024, while one separate 2017 record is marked `Tidak Berlaku`. Writers must not resolve this catalog inconsistency by preference or date alone.
- Exact grades, tolerances, test values, coating thicknesses, acceptance levels, design checks, weld variables, bolt tightening values, sampling plans, and service-life claims require the current full standard plus the governing Indonesian/project context.
- Record the standard number **and edition** in an RFQ, certificate review, drawing, ITP, or article example. Recheck status when outlining begins and again before publication.

- **Incorporation:** BES-02-A01–A06, BES-13-A01, BES-13-A03–A05, and BES-14-A01–A06 must name edition and application source, distinguish catalog evidence from full-text evidence, and route exact criteria to G-01/G-02.

### KR-02 — Steel-product vocabulary is shape- and stage-based

- **Sources:** [ISO 6929:2013 — Steel products — Vocabulary](https://www.iso.org/standard/52949.html) and [ISO ICS classification for iron and steel products](https://www.iso.org/files/live/sites/isoorg/files/archive/pdf/en/international_classification_for_standards.pdf)
- **Grade:** B-STD — official ISO abstract and classification.
- **Purpose:** Give terminology articles a defensible taxonomy without treating Indonesian seller labels as grade evidence.
- **Summary:** ISO 6929 defines steel-product terms by stage of manufacture, shape/dimensions, and appearance. ISO's classification separates flat products, bars/rods, wire, profiles, pipes/tubes, castings, and other families.
- **Grounded facts:** Product-form vocabulary describes form and manufacturing stage; it does not establish chemistry, grade, tolerance, coating, or suitability. Pipe/tube, profile, plate/sheet, bar, wire, grating, and fabricated product require separate evidence chains.
- **Incorporation:** BES-01-A01, A05–A06; BES-04-A01–A06; BES-05-A01–A05; BES-06-A01, A04–A05; and BES-07-A01–A05 should lead with form and boundary, then send grade identity to BES-02.
- **Limits/recheck:** The abstract does not provide every definition. Indonesian trade vocabulary may differ; verify the governing product standard, datasheet, marking, and certificate before assigning a formal product term.

### KR-03 — Mechanical properties depend on test method and sample identity

- **Sources:** [ISO 6892-1:2019 — tensile testing at room temperature](https://www.iso.org/standard/78322.html), [ISO 377:2017 — steel samples and test pieces](https://www.iso.org/standard/72529.html), and [ISO 377:2017/Amd 1:2025](https://www.iso.org/standard/89449.html)
- **Grade:** B-STD — official ISO abstracts and amendment record.
- **Purpose:** Prevent a property name or isolated test value from being presented as proof of grade, whole-stock conformity, or structural capacity.
- **Summary:** ISO 6892-1 defines a tensile test method and properties determinable at room temperature. ISO 377 covers identification, location, and preparation of samples/test pieces for sections, bars, rod, flat, and tubular products, while allowing the order or product standard to govern when requirements differ.
- **Grounded facts:** A reported property must stay linked to specimen source, orientation/location, preparation, method, condition, unit, and product/heat identity. A method defines how to test; the governing material/product specification defines required values and disposition.
- **Incorporation:** BES-01-A02–A04, BES-02-A02/A06, BES-03-A01/A03, BES-08-A02–A04, and BES-14-A03 should annotate the evidence chain rather than publish generic pass/fail values.
- **Limits/recheck:** Full method, product standard, sampling plan, amendment, laboratory scope, uncertainty, and actual report are required for any acceptance claim; resolve through G-01/G-13.

### KR-04 — Structural steel delivery scope is product-specific

- **Sources:** [ISO 630-1:2021 — general delivery conditions for hot-rolled structural products](https://www.iso.org/standard/73841.html)
- **Grade:** B-STD — official ISO abstract.
- **Purpose:** Separate a general structural-steel family from sheet/strip, tube, and the specific grades in other parts of the standard.
- **Summary:** The public scope covers general technical delivery conditions for hot-rolled flat and long products used principally as general-purpose structural steels and intended for welded or bolted structures. It explicitly directs sheet/strip and tubular products to other standards and says specific requirements sit in individual ISO 630 parts.
- **Grounded facts:** A structural use label is not one universal product specification. Form, standard part, grade, supply condition, dimensions, and order requirements must remain linked.
- **Incorporation:** BES-01-A03–A04, BES-02-A01/A04, BES-04, BES-05-A01–A03, BES-08-A01/A06, and BES-13-A01 should use a specification hierarchy rather than a generic “structural steel” equivalence.
- **Limits/recheck:** Do not extract grades, chemistry, mechanical values, tolerances, or weldability from the abstract. Obtain the applicable ISO/SNI/ASTM/JIS product document and project specification through G-01/G-02.

### KR-07 — Inspection documents are order-linked, not self-authenticating

- **Sources:** [ISO 10474:2013 — inspection documents](https://www.iso.org/standard/53736.html) and [ISO/IEC 17025:2017 — laboratory competence](https://www.iso.org/standard/66912.html)
- **Grade:** B-STD — official ISO abstracts.
- **Purpose:** Structure certificate reading, material traceability, laboratory review, and handover evidence.
- **Summary:** ISO 10474 defines types of inspection documents supplied to a purchaser according to order requirements and is used with general delivery standards. ISO/IEC 17025 addresses competence, impartiality, and consistent operation of testing/calibration laboratories.
- **Grounded facts:** An inspection document must be reconciled to the order, producer, product, heat/batch, dimensions, tests, and physical markings. Laboratory accreditation/competence is relevant to confidence in results but does not itself prove that a particular item conforms.
- **Incorporation:** BES-02-A02–A06, BES-09-A06, BES-13-A01/A05, BES-14-A01–A03/A06, and BES-16-A04 should use document-to-material reconciliation and an evidence-gap register.
- **Limits/recheck:** Do not name document types, required signatories, sampling, or acceptance rules without the current full standard/order. Verify issuer, accreditation scope, method, report authorization, and chain of identity through G-03/G-13.

### KR-08 — Nominal size, dimensions, and mass are different evidence questions

- **Sources:** [ISO 4200:1991 — steel-tube dimensions and masses](https://www.iso.org/standard/9985.html) and [ASME B36.10-2022 — welded and seamless wrought steel pipe](https://www.asme.org/codes-standards/find-codes-standards/welded-and-seamless-wrought-steel-pipe)
- **Grade:** B-STD — official standards-body scope pages.
- **Purpose:** Ground dimensional-table and theoretical-mass articles without confusing nominal labels, measured geometry, invoice basis, and service rating.
- **Summary:** ISO 4200 provides general tables of plain-end tube dimensions and masses per unit length. ASME B36.10 standardizes dimensions for welded and seamless wrought steel pipe. Neither public scope establishes that a particular pipe is suitable for a real fluid, pressure, temperature, structural, or connection system.
- **Grounded facts:** A theoretical mass is tied to a referenced geometry, length, density assumption, rounding rule, and product table. Receiving measurement and actual weighing are separate checks.
- **Incorporation:** BES-03-A01–A06, BES-07-A02–A03/A06, BES-13-A02–A03, and BES-14-A01 should show an assumption register and reconcile table, measurement, weighbridge, piece count, and contractual basis.
- **Limits/recheck:** Do not publish table values, tolerances, density, schedule equivalence, or invoice entitlement without the full governing standard and supply terms. Resolve G-04/G-16.

### KR-09 — Open sections and hollow structural sections are not trade-name substitutes

- **Sources:** [AISC HSS resources](https://www.aisc.org/aisc/solutions-center/hss/) and [AISC structural-product EDI naming convention](https://www.aisc.org/globalassets/aisc/manual/v15.0-shapes-database/naming-convention-for-structural-steel-products-for-use-in-electronic-data-interchange-edi.pdf)

- **Purpose:** Support profile anatomy and naming comparisons while preventing U.S. designations from being treated as Indonesian supply or design rules.

- **Limits/recheck:** U.S. ASTM/AISC examples are illustrative only. Use current Indonesian/project profile tables, complete standards, mill data, and licensed design through G-04/G-06.

### KR-10 — Pipe geometry, manufacturing specification, and service design are separate

- **Sources:** [API announcement for API Spec 5L 47th edition](https://www.api.org/products-and-services/standards/important-standards-announcements/api-announces-47th-edition-of-foundational-line-pipe-standard-5l), [ASME B36.10-2022 scope](https://www.asme.org/codes-standards/find-codes-standards/welded-and-seamless-wrought-steel-pipe), and [ISO 4200 scope](https://www.iso.org/standard/9985.html)
- **Grade:** B-STD/C-GOV — official standards developers; foreign scope.
- **Purpose:** Stop articles from deriving pressure rating, fluid compatibility, or structural suitability from schedule, color, seam, or galvanizing.

- **Incorporation:** BES-07-A01–A06, BES-08-A01/A06, BES-10-A01, BES-13-A01, and BES-14-A01 must show separate geometry/product/service columns.
- **Limits/recheck:** Foreign API/ASME/ISO documents are not Indonesian law. Obtain the specified full edition, actual certificate, design code, fluid/exposure data, fittings/joints, tests, and responsible engineer approval through G-01/G-16.

### KR-12 — Welding quality is a managed special process

### KR-15 — Cutting and fabrication tolerances apply only when invoked

- **Sources:** [ISO 9013:2017 — thermal cuts](https://www.iso.org/standard/60321.html), [ISO 9013:2017/Amd 1:2024](https://www.iso.org/standard/87851.html), and [ISO 13920:2023 — general tolerances for welded constructions](https://www.iso.org/standard/86032.html)

### KR-16 — Corrosion environment precedes paint-system selection

- **Sources:** [ISO 12944-2:2017 — classification of environments](https://www.iso.org/standard/64834.html), [ISO 12944-5:2019 — protective paint systems](https://www.iso.org/standard/77795.html), and [AMPP Corrosion Basics catalog](https://content.ampp.org/books/book/12/Corrosion-Basics-An-Introduction)
- **Grade:** B-STD/C-GOV — official ISO abstracts and recognized corrosion-industry reference.
- **Purpose:** Ground corrosion mechanisms, exposure surveys, and coating-system comparisons.
- **Summary:** ISO 12944-2 addresses environment/corrosivity classification, including atmospheric, immersed/buried, and special stresses. ISO 12944-5 describes paint-system types and selection guidance in relation to environment, preparation, and intended durability. - **Incorporation:** BES-08-A01/A05, BES-11-A01–A06, BES-14-A05, BES-15-A04, and BES-16-A02/A03 use an exposure record and system layers rather than generic product claims.
- **Limits/recheck:** ISO 12944-2 and -5 are marked for revision on their catalog pages. Exact categories, preparation, system, dry-film thickness, test, durability, repair, and service-life claims require current full parts, manufacturer data, and site facts through G-09.

### KR-17 — Galvanizing scope changes by product and process

- **Sources:** [ISO 1461:2022 — fabricated articles](https://www.iso.org/standard/81435.html), [ISO 14713-2:2019 — design for hot-dip galvanizing](https://www.iso.org/standard/71136.html), and [ISO 1460:2020 — gravimetric coating-mass method](https://www.iso.org/standard/79581.html)
- **Grade:** B-STD — official ISO abstracts.
- **Purpose:** Separate after-fabrication hot-dip galvanizing from continuously galvanized sheet/wire/mesh, automatic pipe/tube plants, hardware standards, and extra paint layers.
- **Summary:** ISO 1461:2022 covers general properties and tests for coatings applied by dipping fabricated iron/steel articles and explicitly excludes several continuously or automatically galvanized product families and after-treatments/additional coatings. ISO 14713-2 covers design guidance for articles galvanized after fabrication.
- **Grounded facts:** The same word `galvanis` can refer to materially different base product, coating route, and governing standard. A test method's scope does not state that an actual coating passed.
- **Incorporation:** BES-04-A05, BES-05-A02/A06, BES-07-A03, BES-11-A04–A06, and BES-14-A05 must identify product form, coating process, applicable product/coating standard, inspection, and repair.
- **Limits/recheck:** Do not transfer ISO 1461 values or acceptance rules to sheet, wire, mesh, pipe, fasteners, or Indonesian procurement without the applicable full standard and order. Resolve G-01/G-05/G-09.

### KR-18 — Galvanizing requires fabrication and inspection coordination

- **Sources:** [AGA venting and draining guidance](https://galvanizeit.org/knowledgebase/article/venting-and-draining), [AGA design/fabrication practices](https://galvanizeit.org/aess-for-specifiers/design-details-and-fabrication-best-practices-for-hdg), and [AGA inspection guide page](https://galvanizeit.org/education-and-resources/publications/inspection-of-hot-dip-galvanized-steel-products)
- **Grade:** D-MFR — American Galvanizers Association guidance; North American process/specification context.
- **Purpose:** Provide practical questions for galvanizer coordination, coating inspection, transport damage, and repair.
- **Summary:** AGA explains that fabricated articles must permit cleaning solutions, air, and zinc to enter/escape and drain, and that coating inspection includes more than visual appearance. It repeatedly recommends communication with the galvanizer before fabrication.
- **Grounded facts:** Enclosed/overlapped geometry, vent/drain paths, lift orientation, welds, material chemistry, surface contamination, handling, appearance expectations, inspection, and repair interact. Final hole size/location and structural effects require the responsible designer and galvanizer.
- **Incorporation:** BES-09-A03/A05, BES-11-A04–A06, BES-14-A05, BES-15-A02, and BES-16-A03 use early coordination and damage/repair records.
- **Limits/recheck:** Do not import AGA/ASTM dimensions, acceptance, or repair values into an Indonesian project. Obtain the actual galvanizer's written review, governing standard, product data, approved drawings, and coating specification through G-05/G-09/G-10.

### KR-19 — Indonesian construction and workplace K3 sources

- **Grade:** A-ID — official Indonesian JDIH records.
- **Purpose:** Anchor fabrication, lifting, transport, erection, work-at-height, and workplace-exposure outlines in current Indonesian sources.
- **Summary:** The official records mark these regulations `Berlaku` and identify distinct scopes for construction safety management, work environment, lifting/transport equipment, work at height, and power/production equipment.
- **Grounded facts:** Steel work crosses multiple K3 scopes; one generic checklist cannot replace employer duties, competent personnel, equipment records, site risk assessment, work method, permits, emergency planning, and supervision.
- **Incorporation:** BES-09-A01, BES-10-A01, BES-15-A01–A06, BES-16-A05–A06, and relevant BES-14 ITP/handover articles must link hazards to responsible controls and stop-work/escalation routes.
- **Limits/recheck:** Titles/status do not supply the operative clauses or prove applicability to a specific employer, site, machine, lift, or task. Read current consolidated texts and site documents with competent K3/legal review through G-12.

### KR-20 — Welding and thermal cutting create health and safety hazards

- **Sources:** [NIOSH welding fumes and manganese](https://www.cdc.gov/niosh/welding/about/index.html), [OSHA welding/cutting/brazing hazards](https://www.osha.gov/welding-cutting-brazing/hazards-solutions), and [NIOSH local-exhaust engineering-control evaluation](https://www.cdc.gov/niosh/engcontrols/ecd/detail44.html)
- **Grade:** C-GOV — United States government health/safety guidance.
- **Purpose:** Give K3 articles a hazard map without converting U.S. exposure limits or controls into Indonesian prescriptions.
- **Summary:** NIOSH and OSHA identify fumes/gases, UV radiation, burns, fire/explosion, electrical shock, noise, eye/skin injury, and metal-specific hazards. NIOSH notes exposure changes with process, consumable, base/coated metal, duration/intensity, and confined-space conditions.
- **Grounded facts:** A welding risk assessment needs process, base/coating/contamination, consumable, ventilation, work position/enclosure, adjacent workers, hot-work/fire conditions, electrical/gas equipment, exposure assessment, and emergency controls.
- **Incorporation:** BES-10-A01–A05 and BES-15-A05 use hazard prompts and route exposure/control selection to Indonesian legal/K3 evidence in KR-19.
- **Limits/recheck:** Do not copy U.S. limits, respirator choices, ventilation rates, or PPE as Indonesian/site requirements. Competent industrial hygiene and K3 review plus current Indonesian limits/procedures are required through G-12.

### KR-21 — Handling and storage controls depend on form, mass, support, and coating

- **Sources:** [OSHA 1926.250 material-storage requirements](https://www.osha.gov/laws-regs/regulations/standardnumber/1926/1926.250), [LYSAGHT product care and storage](https://lysaght.com/support-technical/support/installation/product-care-and-storage-installation), and Indonesian lifting-equipment source [Permenaker 8/2020](https://jdih.kemnaker.go.id/peraturan/detail/1668/peraturan-menteri-nomor-8-tahun-2020)
- **Grade:** A-ID/C-GOV/D-MFR — Indonesian legal record, U.S. regulator, and product-specific manufacturer guidance.
- **Purpose:** Support receiving, transport, lifting-input, storage-layout, moisture, and damage-record articles.
- **Summary:** OSHA's foreign rule illustrates stable stacking/blocking and segregation concepts, including preventing pipes and bar stock from spreading/tilting. LYSAGHT provides bounded care/storage guidance for coated roofing/cladding products. Indonesian lifting applicability comes from the current Permenaker record, not OSHA or the manufacturer.
- **Grounded facts:** A storage plan needs verified mass/geometry/center of gravity, stable ground/rack/supports, rolling/spreading prevention, stack access, aisles, drainage/ventilation, segregation, marking visibility, coating/contact protection, weather/condensation, inspection, and quarantine.
- **Incorporation:** BES-14-A01, BES-15-A01–A04, and BES-16-A01 use product-form-specific layouts and condition records, with no universal stack height or support spacing.
- **Limits/recheck:** Foreign/manufacturer guidance is not an Indonesian legal rule or a design for mixed stock. Obtain supplier handling instructions, packaging plan, lift/storage design, site capacity, weather/exposure, and competent approval through G-05/G-12.

### KR-22 — Chain of custody supports claims but does not prove product characteristics

- **Sources:** [ISO 22095:2020 — chain-of-custody framework](https://www.iso.org/standard/72532.html), [ISO 22095:2020/Amd 1:2026](https://www.iso.org/standard/90678.html), and [ISO's 2026 chain-of-custody update](https://www.iso.org/cms/live/live/en/sites/isoorg/contents/news/2026/03/new-iso-standards-bring-clarity.html)
- **Grade:** B-STD — official ISO abstract, amendment, and update.
- **Purpose:** Clarify material identity, sustainability claims, supplier evidence, and auditable handover.
- **Summary:** ISO 22095 provides generic chain-of-custody terminology/models across materials and says the framework alone cannot support a claim about a product's characteristics or production conditions. ISO published an amendment and model-specific parts in 2026.
- **Grounded facts:** Physical identity/segregation, mass-balance, and administrative claims are different models. An auditable claim needs a defined characteristic, system boundary, input/output records, transfer rules, responsible parties, and no double counting.
- **Incorporation:** BES-02-A03/A05, BES-09-A06, BES-13-A02/A05/A06, BES-14-A06, and BES-16-A04/A06 should distinguish physical heat/item traceability from environmental or recycled-content claims.
- **Limits/recheck:** ISO 22095 is not steel-specific and is under review. Obtain the claim scheme, current full standard/2026 parts, supplier records, assurance scope, and transaction evidence through G-03/G-15/G-17.

### KR-23 — Sampling and laboratory competence do not define project acceptance by themselves

- **Sources:** [ISO 2859-1:2026 — lot-by-lot attribute sampling](https://www.iso.org/standard/85464.html), [ISO 28590:2017 — introduction to the ISO 2859 series](https://www.iso.org/standard/64622.html), and [ISO/IEC 17025:2017](https://www.iso.org/standard/66912.html)
- **Grade:** B-STD — official ISO abstracts/current edition pages.
- **Purpose:** Keep receiving inspection, ITP, supplier audit, and test-plan articles from inventing sample sizes or treating one result as a lot decision.
- **Summary:** ISO 2859-1:2026 is the new edition for specified acceptance-sampling schemes; ISO 28590 explains that different ISO 2859 parts address different situations. ISO/IEC 17025 addresses laboratory competence, not the product acceptance criterion.
- **Grounded facts:** A test plan must define lot/population, characteristic, method, sample selection, sample identity, frequency/size, criterion source, decision rule, responsibilities, records, and nonconformance route.
- **Incorporation:** BES-02-A05/A06, BES-13-A05, BES-14-A01–A05, and BES-16-A04 use a test-plan template with criterion and disposition owners.
- **Limits/recheck:** Never choose AQL, sample size, switching rule, test extent, uncertainty rule, or release from abstracts. Obtain the full current sampling/product standards and approved ITP through G-01/G-13.

### KR-24 — Existing structures require assessment, not visual grade assignment

- **Sources:** [ISO 13822:2010 — assessment of existing structures](https://www.iso.org/standard/46556.html)
- **Grade:** B-STD — official ISO abstract; international framework.
- **Purpose:** Ground inspection, change-of-use, retrofit, repair, and reuse articles in an assessment process.
- **Summary:** ISO 13822 provides general assessment principles for existing buildings, bridges, and industrial structures and identifies triggers including change of use/design life, reliability checks, deterioration such as corrosion/fatigue, and accidental damage.
- **Grounded facts:** Existing-condition work begins with purpose, consequences, documents, survey, material identity, geometry, actions/use history, alterations, deterioration/damage, monitoring/tests, analysis, temporary controls, and reviewed disposition.
- **Incorporation:** BES-12-A04–A06 and BES-16-A01–A05 should build an evidence-gap matrix and state that appearance cannot establish grade, weldability, remaining capacity, or reuse suitability.
- **Limits/recheck:** ISO 13822 says additional detail is needed for fire and earthquake and may need material/national adaptation. Use current Indonesian rules, project-specific calculations, and competent assessment through G-06/G-14.

### KR-25 — Fire exposure is a structural-system assessment problem

- **Sources:** [NIST best-practice guidance for structural fire resistance](https://www.nist.gov/publications/best-practice-guidelines-structural-fire-resistance-design-concrete-and-steel-buildings), [NIST white paper on fire behavior of steel structures](https://www.nist.gov/publications/white-paper-fire-behavior-steel-structures), and [ISO 24679-1:2019 — performance of structures in fire](https://www.iso.org/standard/72893.html)
- **Grade:** B-STD/C-GOV — official ISO abstract and U.S. government technical reports.
- **Purpose:** Support fire-design and post-fire evidence articles without publishing a generic steel temperature or reoccupation rule.
- **Summary:** The sources treat fire performance through fire scenario/exposure, thermal response, structural response, protection, connections, system behavior, objectives, and engineering assessment. NIST explicitly does not present its guidance as a step-by-step universal design procedure.
- **Grounded facts:** Post-fire work needs incident history, duration/location indicators, firefighting/cooling, protection condition, deformation, connections, material/fabrication records, temporary controls, survey, sampling/testing rationale, and competent disposition.
- **Incorporation:** BES-08-A05, BES-12-A05, BES-14-A03, and BES-16-A02/A04 preserve emergency-authority and fire/structural-engineer gates.
- **Limits/recheck:** Do not infer fire rating, retained properties, safety, repair, or reuse from appearance, color, or one temperature estimate. Resolve Indonesian fire/building rules and project assessment through G-06/G-11/G-14.

### KR-26 — Fatigue and fracture depend on load history and critical details

- **Sources:** [FHWA fatigue/fracture reference manual](https://www.fhwa.dot.gov/bridge/steel/pubs/nhi16016.pdf), [FHWA bridge-inspection resource hub](https://www.fhwa.dot.gov/bridge/inspection/), and [FHWA fatigue-crack NDE program](https://www.fhwa.dot.gov/publications/ndec/ndecnews.cfm)
- **Grade:** C-GOV — U.S. government bridge guidance; foreign and application-specific.
- **Purpose:** Ground fatigue/fracture and defect-escalation articles while preventing bridge guidance from becoming a generic building rule.
- **Summary:** FHWA resources connect repetitive loading, fatigue-prone details, connections/welds, inspection access, visual inspection, selected NDE, findings, and follow-up. They emphasize trained inspection and detail-specific evaluation.
- **Grounded facts:** Fatigue/fracture review requires asset/detail identity, load/use history and changes, stress/detail context, material/fabrication/weld history, corrosion/damage, prior findings/repairs, access, monitoring, and competent evaluation.
- **Incorporation:** BES-08-A02/A04, BES-12-A04, BES-14-A04, and BES-16-A02/A05 use a critical-detail history, not a symptom-to-repair shortcut.
- **Limits/recheck:** FHWA bridge rules are not Indonesian building requirements. Do not estimate remaining life, inspection interval, criticality, or repair from these public sources; resolve through G-06/G-08/G-14.

### KR-27 — Reuse is not the same as recycling

- **Sources:** [worldsteel circular economy](https://worldsteel.org/wider-sustainability/circular-economy/), [worldsteel circular-economy publication](https://worldsteel.org/media/publications/steel-permanent-circular-economy/), and [AISC recycling/deconstruction overview](https://www.aisc.org/aisc/sustainability/recycling/)
- **Grade:** C-GOV — recognized steel-industry bodies; global/U.S. context.
- **Purpose:** Define reduce, reuse, remanufacture, and recycle while preserving structural reuse and waste-law gates.
- **Summary:** worldsteel distinguishes reuse without significantly changing physical form from recycling by remelting scrap. AISC illustrates deconstruction and refabrication of structural members, while recycling remaining material as scrap.
- **Grounded facts:** Reuse can retain more product value but requires provenance, condition, geometry, material/weldability evidence, alterations/exposure history, new use, testing rationale, and design approval. Recycling does not preserve a member's identity or certify recycled-content/environmental claims.
- **Incorporation:** BES-13-A06 and BES-16-A04–A06 must separate reuse dossier, scrap sorting, contamination/waste route, transaction receipts, and claim verification.
- **Limits/recheck:** Industry sustainability claims are not project LCAs, material certification, structural approval, or Indonesian waste compliance. Obtain project evidence, licensed assessment, current waste rules, and authorized routes through G-14/G-17.

### KR-28 — Maintenance needs an asset baseline and decision system

- **Sources:** [ISO 55001:2024 — asset-management-system requirements](https://www.iso.org/standard/83054.html) and [ISO 13822:2010](https://www.iso.org/standard/46556.html)
- **Grade:** B-STD — official ISO pages.
- **Purpose:** Support asset register, baseline inspection, recurring condition review, intervention choice, and handover.
- **Summary:** ISO 55001 frames asset management as a system connecting objectives, decisions, lifecycle, performance, risk, and expenditure. ISO 13822 supplies the assessment boundary when condition, changed use, deterioration, or damage creates a structural question.
- **Grounded facts:** A useful steel-asset baseline links unique identity/location/function to drawings/dossier, material/fabrication evidence, geometry, coating, connections, environment, condition/photos, defects, access, actions, inspections, changes, decisions, and next review.
- **Incorporation:** BES-14-A06, BES-16-A01–A05, and BES-13-A06 use an asset/evidence register and escalation logic; a complete file is not itself a safety certificate.
- **Limits/recheck:** The asset-management abstract does not prescribe inspection interval, defect limit, repair, or structural acceptance. The owner and competent disciplines must define the risk-based plan using project facts through G-14/G-18.

## Downstream stop conditions

Article outlines and drafts must stop and escalate when any of the following appears:

- the grade, heat, product standard, certificate, or physical marking does not reconcile;
- a proposed substitution changes form, grade, edition, supply condition, dimensions, coating, welding, inspection, service, or design assumptions;
- a load-bearing, pressure-containing, fire-exposed, cyclic/fatigue-sensitive, corroded, cracked, deformed, modified, or unknown item needs a fitness/safety decision;
- welding or repair lacks an approved application code, WPS/qualification, material identity, competent coordination, or inspection route;
- lifting, storage, erection, hot work, work at height, or deconstruction lacks a site-specific competent plan and emergency controls;
- exact requirements are available only in a paywalled/full standard not held by the writer;
- a manufacturer or foreign source conflicts with Indonesian law, the project specification, drawings, or responsible professional;
- the writer is asked to publish current price, stock, supplier endorsement, conformity, service life, capacity, acceptance, or legal compliance without dated primary evidence.

## Refresh policy

Recheck before outlining and again before publication when:

- BSN, Kemenperin, PUPR, or Kemnaker changes a record, regulation, status, amendment, or referenced standard;
- ISO/AWS/API/ASME publishes a new edition, amendment, corrigendum, or withdrawal (several cited ISO pages already show review/revision activity);
- the project changes product form, jurisdiction, structure/system type, service, exposure, fabrication route, supplier, or intended claim;
- a direct link no longer resolves or a manufacturer revises its product/process guidance;
- actual certificates, test reports, drawings, specifications, site surveys, calculations, ITPs, or operating/inspection histories become available.
