# Topical Authority — besi.co.id

## Role and boundary

`besi.co.id` should be an Indonesian, national-scope reference and procurement-support hub for iron and steel materials: terminology, metallurgy, grades, certificates, product forms, dimensions, selection, fabrication, welding, corrosion protection, structural interfaces, testing, purchasing, transport, storage, safe handling, maintenance, reuse, and recycling. Its defensible role is helping buyers, designers, fabricators, contractors, inspectors, and asset owners ask for the right material and evidence.

The domain should not become a collection of city-swapped “jual” pages. Geography is excluded from this editorial plan. Existing commercial product routes may retain transactional intent after canonical review, while neutral educational articles own reference and decision intent. The site does not replace a licensed structural engineer, welding engineer, metallurgist, inspector, laboratory, K3 professional, manufacturer, or authority. It must not infer grade, capacity, weldability, conformity, or safety from appearance or a trade name.

## Evidence and editorial standards

1. Mark statements as repository observation, sourced fact, formula, worked example, professional judgment, or hypothesis.
2. Use current primary standards and authority/manufacturer/laboratory sources where accessible. Record designation, edition, jurisdiction, access date, and whether the document is normative, supplier-specific, or explanatory.
3. Never infer grade, chemistry, mechanical properties, coating, weldability, capacity, pressure rating, fire behavior, origin, or conformity from color, appearance, magnet response, sparks, product name, or seller statement alone.
4. Grade/equivalence content requires both governing specifications, edition-aware requirement comparison, product form/supply condition, and competent approval. A crosswalk is not proof of interchangeability.
5. Quantity content states formulas, units, dimensions, tolerances, density assumptions, cutting waste, and whether mass is theoretical, measured, or invoiced. Worked values are labeled examples.
6. Fabrication and welding content requires verified material identity, approved drawings, qualified procedures and personnel where applicable, calibrated equipment, inspection hold points, repair control, and traceability preservation.
7. Corrosion content requires environment, substrate/material pair, surface condition, preparation, complete coating/galvanizing system, application records, inspection method, and maintenance assumptions before durability claims.
8. Structural content has a visible **licensed-engineer evidence gate**: site, use, actions, load path, governing design basis, material records, section and connection data, stability, fire/temperature where relevant, temporary states, and reviewed calculations/drawings.
9. Procurement content separates technical compliance, commercial terms, logistics, warranty, and lifecycle assumptions. Do not publish live prices or supplier rankings without date, scope, sampling method, and disclosure.
10. QA/testing content distinguishes mill certificate, inspection certificate, third-party result, field identification, and independent test. Name sampling, specimen, method, laboratory competence, uncertainty, and acceptance owner.
11. Transport/storage/K3 content requires verified mass and center of gravity, package and lifting information, route/site constraints, stable stacking/restraint, coating/weather protection, and a site-specific competent lifting/work plan. Generic limits are prohibited.
12. Reuse content treats unknown or deteriorated steel as unverified until history, dimensions, material identity, defects, connections, environment, and competent assessment support a defined new use.
13. Safety-critical symptoms trigger isolation/escalation according to the responsible competent person’s plan; an article never declares a structure, lift, weld, pressure system, or material safe.
14. Every safety-, standards-, engineering-, testing-, or price-sensitive page shows author, reviewer, sources, last technical review date, and correction route.
15. Indonesian writing should use plain language, define unavoidable trade terms, show decision tables/diagrams/checklists where useful, and avoid generic SEO padding.
16. One article owns one primary intent. Review title, slug, H1, outline, and real queries; merge or redraw pages when same-domain overlap emerges.
